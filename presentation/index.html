The EPR Paradox
  • Given a source of maximally entangled particles $ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \ket{00} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \ket{11}$ measuring the state of 1 particle immediately determines the state of the other particle.
  • We must give an interpretation to this spooky action at a distance.
  • Perhaps the particles decide in advance which state they will be in.
Bell's Theorem
"No physical theory of local hidden variables can ever reproduce all of the predictions of quantum mechanics."
  • Locality: information cannot travel faster than $c$.
  • Realism: quantum states have well-defined properties, independent of measurement.
A simple counting argument
  • Consider a set of elements $E$ s.t. $ \forall e \in E$ either $p_i(e) = 0 $ or $p_i(e) = 1$ for $ i = 1, 2, 3$
  • Since we have 3 (hidden) properties we have $2^3$ possible states for each element.
  • We cannot see these states but we can perform single tests on one of the properties.
A simple counting argument (1)
  • Alice and Bob set up to crack the mistery by measuring all possible combinations of 2 different states.
  • They take an element from $E$, perform the test and note down when they agree on the result and when they disagree.

Bell's Inequality
$$ N(p_1, \neg p_2) + N(p_2, \neg p_3) \geq N( p_1, \neg p_3) $$
  • This inequality is true in general for any set of element on which we define some properties.
  • In fact it's not strictly related to QM, it's a purely logical argument.
  • However experiments on quantum systems result in this inequality being violated.
Bell Test Experiments
  • Devising a loop-free experiment to test the violation of these inequalities is quite hard.
  • The principle however is quite simple. A pair source sends 1 element ( a photon, an electron ) to Alice and 1 to Bob.
  • What they have to do are just measurements of the state (polarization or spin) of their elements.
Bell Test Experiments (1)
  • What is observed in the case of spin is that the measurement along one direction immediately determines that the entangled companion will have the opposite spin if measured along that same direction
  • For entangled photons what we instead observe is that measuring along the same direction alwats yields the same result.
Bell Test Experiments (2)
  • Furthermore the probabilities of observing one element in some state in-between in the chosen basis is coherent with the predictions of QM.
  • For photons in particular it's easy to measure their polarization with a filter.
Quantum Key Distribution
  • We can use the polarization of photons to perform Evesdropper-detecting key exchange.
Quantum Key Distribution (1)
  • Alice picks any two different basis, say, $\ket{\uparrow}$ and $\ket{\rightarrow} $ and $\ket{\nearrow }$ and $\ket{\nwarrow} $
  • Everytime she transmits a photon she chooses one of the basis at random.
  • Bob also chooses a basis at random and measures the polarization of the photon.
Quantum Key Distribution (2)
  • After a number of photons exchanged they communicate the list of basis chosen for each measurement.
  • On average they're going to agree on 50% of the transmitted bits.
  • These bits will be used to compute the symmetrical keys. The others will be discarded.
Quantum Key Distribution (3)
  • Consider what happens if Eve wants to eavesdrop the quantum channel.
  • She does not know which basis Alice will choose each time so this woman in the middle attack is doomed to fail since when she'll send the new photons to Bob she will only choose the original basis 50% of the time.
  • But now the agreement rate for Alice and Bob will drop to 25%, exposing Eve's eavesdropping.
A quantum leap back
  • We can build a simple system to observe the violation of Bell's Inequalities in action.
  • All we need is a source of photons and some polarizer filters.
  • We can use our built-in photon detector devices to observe the phenomenon but in order to take quantitative measurements we'll use an external system.
A brief history of quanta
  • For a black body, the thermal radiation, according to the classical theory of EM and TD should be
    • $$ I(\lambda) = \frac{2\pi c}{\lambda^4} kT $$
A brief history of quanta (1)
  • The previous equation is known as the Rayleigh-Jeans formula and while it kinda holds for longer wavelengths it fails catastrophically for frequencies near the UV.
  • This is undesirable since we know that the theories from which it's derived are sound and well tested.
A brief history of quanta (2)
  • Planck proposed that an oscillating atom can only absorb or emit energy in quanta $ E = h \nu$.
  • This changes the derivation of the blackbody emission equation since we're now dealing with discrete variables.
  • The correct equation is thus:
    • $$ I(\lambda) = \frac{2\pi hc^2}{\lambda^5} \frac{1}{\exp{\frac{hc}{\lambda kT}}-1} $$
The photoelectric effect
  • Electrons are very picky! They won't budge for less than $\varphi$
  • The remaining energy thus become $$K_{max} = hf - \varphi $$
The photovoltaic effect
  • When the electrons remain confined in the material itself this is usually referred as the photovoltaic effect.
  • Main applications in solar panels and photodetectors

The system
  • The circuit is extremely simple. An LED shines light through some orientable polarizers.
  • At the receiving end a reverse biased LED is connected to the base of a Darlington Transistor.
The system (1)
  • Due to the photoelectric effect when light shines on the p-n junction an electron-hole pair generates a small current.
  • The current goes through the base of the transistor opening it.
  • Since we're measuring between the collector and emitter the measuring point will be pulled to ground.
The system (2)
  • Rotating the filters changes the amount of light according to the predictions of QM.
  • Since we have 4 filters we label them $ \ket{f_0} $ to $\ket{f_3}$.
  • We only perform measurement along 1 axis for each filter configuration so we don't bother labeling the other orthogonal axis.
The system (3)
  • It follows that the amount of light passing through, wrt to the configuration where the filters are all aligned is:
$$ \prod_{i = 0}^{2} { \bk{f_i}{f_{i+1}} }^2 $$
The software

  • In the program we can set the position of the filters and the computed value for the voltage will be output along with the real value and the delta.
